What a year 2023 has been

As 2023 ends, I'm still mentally processing what a year it has been. There have been many highs this year such as graduating from U of T with an Honours Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and Mathematics, joining TELUS as part of their Graduate Technology Leadership Program and recently traveling to Vancouver with friends.

However, the year has not been always all ups as there were many low points where I questioned my skills and let imposter syndrome slowly creep in during the process. If there's one thing 2023 has taught me is that more work is needed on self-care and confidence.

Side-tangent aside, I am still trying to build my own career path and getting use to working full-time . One piece is networking whether it is meeting new people in the office, setting up coffee chats and even reconnecting with old colleagues during my time from co-op. I am constantly learning new soft skills or technical skills as part of my career tool belt. I'm also using this time after University to focus on what is it that I want next for me and how to achieve that vision.
